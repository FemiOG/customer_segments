Feature Transformation

The total variance explained for two and four dimensions of the data from PCA is accurately reported. The first four dimensions are interpreted as a representation of customer spending with justification.

You correctly reported the total explained variance ratio in the first two and first four principal component. Here we are trying to help you understand the relationship between the original features and the principal components.

But here we only need to focus on absolute weights and its direction. Positive and negative can be exchangeable here.

You may notice that in the first principal component, Detergents_Paper, Grocery, Milk have significant feature weights showing that they have strong correlation. The following three principal components are also correlated: the second has large weights in Fresh、Frozen、Delicatessen meaning these three are correlated; the third is similar to the second principal component except in Fresh purchase and the fourth is also similar to the second principal component except in Delicatessen.

In summary, we can assume that the first principal component and the second, third and fourth together (2+3+4) can represent two customer categories. You may also refer to the Biplot below to see whether it complies with your conclusion.

http://setosa.io/ev/principal-component-analysis/ also have a PCA visualization page.

PCA has been properly implemented and applied to both the scaled data and scaled sample data for the two-dimensional case in code.

You also noticed that the values for the first two dimensions remains unchanged compared to a PCA transformation in six dimensions.
